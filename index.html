Research method

How SpyWatch should grow

1. Source chain

Start with official records, court documents, agency releases, and established investigative reporting. Mark encyclopedia pages as summaries, not primary proof.

2. Confidence labels

Separate confirmed, high-confidence, medium-confidence, disputed, and low-confidence details. Do not flatten allegations into facts.

3. Defender lesson

Every case needs a practical defensive takeaway: identity assurance, access governance, insider-risk process, travel-pattern awareness, or research-security hygiene.

4. Safety boundary

Do not publish private addresses, private family data, scraped dating profiles, live surveillance methods, intrusion steps, or operational targeting instructions.

SpyWatch is a public-source defensive research archive. Attribution is probabilistic; claims must stay sourced and labeled.
