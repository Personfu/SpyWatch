# Casefile: Evgeny Buryakov / SVR New York economic-intelligence case

## Purpose

This casefile summarizes official public records about the 2015-2016 New York SVR case involving Evgeny Buryakov. It is defensive research material only.

## Public-source anchors

- DOJ Southern District of New York sentencing release, May 25, 2016
- DOJ and court-filed public records
- Reputable press summaries may be used as secondary context

## Summary

The U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York announced that Evgeny Buryakov, also known publicly as Zhenya, was sentenced to 30 months in prison for conspiring to act in the United States as an agent of the Russian Federation without prior notice to the Attorney General.

The DOJ release described Buryakov as working under non-official cover as a banker in Manhattan. It also publicly named Igor Sporyshev and Victor Podobnyy as associated SVR officers who were outside U.S. custody and had held official roles that carried diplomatic immunity while they were in the United States.

## Why it matters for SpyWatch

This case is not the same model as the 2010 family-style illegals case. Its value is that it shows the difference between:

- non-official cover, where a person appears as a private-sector employee;
- official or diplomatic cover, where a person has a formal government role;
- economic-intelligence tasking, where targets may include sanctions, energy, finance, technology, or policy information.

## Defensive lessons

1. Economic intelligence can target ordinary business information, not only classified files.
2. Private-sector institutions should treat geopolitical risk and insider-risk governance as part of security planning.
3. Public case records should be used to build awareness of access patterns, not to imitate activity.
4. Case language should separate convicted conduct from unresolved allegations against others.
