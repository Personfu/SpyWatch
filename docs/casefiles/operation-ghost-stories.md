# Casefile: Operation Ghost Stories

## Purpose

This casefile summarizes public records about the 2010 Russian illegals case for defensive education. It is not a guide to covert activity and does not include private addresses, private family details, live contact details, copied personal photographs, or instructions for locating people.

## Public-source anchors

- FBI: Operation Ghost Stories: Inside the Russian Spy Case
- DOJ: Ten Alleged Secret Agents Arrested in the United States
- FBI Vault: Ghost Stories public release collection

## Summary

Operation Ghost Stories was a long-running FBI counterintelligence investigation into a network publicly described as SVR illegals in the United States. The DOJ announced arrests in June 2010, and the FBI later released public educational material through FOIA channels.

The case is important for SpyWatch because it shows how long-term cover identities, ordinary social life, and access-building can become part of a counterintelligence problem. The FBI public page says the investigation lasted more than a decade and that the people involved appeared to lead ordinary lives while building contacts.

## Defensive lessons

1. Long-running access cultivation can look ordinary at the surface level.
2. Source development, social positioning, and identity history should be analyzed together.
3. Public counterintelligence records should be used for training, not imitation.
4. Attribution notes must distinguish official records, press reporting, and later commentary.
