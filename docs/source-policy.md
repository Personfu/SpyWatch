# Source Policy

SpyWatch uses public, attributable information only.

## Preferred sources

1. Government advisories, indictments, sanctions notices, and official public statements.
2. International public-sector cyber and counterintelligence reporting.
3. Reputable security-vendor research.
4. Established investigative journalism with named evidence and clear sourcing.
5. Academic papers, conference talks, books, and declassified records.

## Confidence levels

- `confirmed` — supported by official public attribution or legal filing.
- `high` — supported by multiple reliable public sources.
- `medium` — supported by credible reporting with unresolved uncertainty.
- `low` — useful lead that requires more corroboration.
- `disputed` — credible sources disagree.

## Writing rules

- Separate facts from analysis.
- Cite sources directly in each profile.
- Use neutral language for allegations.
- Date claims when possible.
- Avoid overclaiming attribution.
- Do not include private personal information.
- Do not include operational misuse instructions.

## Review checklist

Before a profile is considered complete, it should have:

- unique `id`;
- readable `name`;
- country or alignment;
- source list;
- confidence rating;
- summary;
- defensive notes;
- last reviewed date.
