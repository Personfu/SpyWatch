# SpyWatch Source Policy

SpyWatch uses public, attributable information only.

## Accepted sources

Preferred sources:

1. Government cybersecurity advisories, indictments, sanctions notices, and official public statements.
2. International public-sector reporting from allied cyber agencies.
3. Reputable security vendor research.
4. Established investigative journalism with named evidence and clear sourcing.
5. Academic papers, conference talks, and books.
6. Historical records and declassified archives.

## Confidence levels

- `confirmed` — supported by official public attribution or legal filing.
- `high` — supported by multiple reliable public sources.
- `medium` — supported by credible reporting but with unresolved uncertainty.
- `low` — useful lead, but requires more corroboration.
- `disputed` — credible sources disagree.

## Writing rules

- Separate facts from analysis.
- Cite sources directly in each profile.
- Use neutral language for allegations.
- Date claims when possible.
- Avoid overclaiming attribution.
- Do not include private personal information.
- Do not include operational misuse instructions.

## Profile review checklist

Before adding a profile, confirm that it has:

- a unique `id`;
- a readable `name`;
- country or alignment field;
- source list;
- confidence rating;
- summary;
- defensive notes;
- last reviewed date.
