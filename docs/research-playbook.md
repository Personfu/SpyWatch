# Safe Research Playbook

This playbook keeps SpyWatch useful without crossing into harmful or invasive conduct.

## Step 1: Define the research question

Examples:

- What public names are used for this actor?
- What country or service is it associated with in public reporting?
- What sectors appear in reliable sources?
- What defensive lessons repeat across sources?
- What claims are disputed or uncertain?

## Step 2: Collect public sources

Use official advisories, court records, sanctions releases, vendor reports, academic papers, and reputable journalism. Keep links and dates.

## Step 3: Separate names from identity

Vendor names are not always the same as legal identity. Keep aliases as aliases, not proof.

## Step 4: Build a neutral profile

Use `public reporting associates`, `government advisory states`, or `vendor reporting tracks` rather than unsupported certainty.

## Step 5: Extract defensive lessons

Turn public history into controls:

- identity governance;
- endpoint visibility;
- logging coverage;
- cloud audit review;
- backup and recovery testing;
- supplier-risk review;
- executive and high-risk-user protection;
- safe reporting channels for suspicious social approaches.

## Step 6: Review for harm

Remove private details, procedural misuse guidance, and anything that would help someone target an organization or individual.
