# Expanded source standards

SpyWatch needs consistent source handling so case files remain useful, factual, and safe.

## Preferred source order

1. Official records: court records, DOJ releases, FBI releases, CISA advisories, Rewards for Justice pages, and allied government statements.
2. Established reporting: Reuters, AP, BBC, The Guardian, Bellingcat, The Insider, Der Spiegel, La Repubblica, and comparable outlets.
3. Secondary summaries: use only for orientation and for finding stronger sources.

## Confidence labels

- confirmed: supported by a plea, conviction, judgment, or direct official record.
- high: supported by strong official attribution or multiple reliable public sources.
- medium: supported by reputable reporting but limited official detail.
- low: useful as a research lead, not as a firm statement.
- disputed: credible disagreement exists or the evidence has not been publicly tested.

## Required casefile sections

Each casefile should include:

1. Purpose
2. Public-source anchors
3. Summary
4. Public names index when necessary
5. Why it matters for SpyWatch
6. Defensive lessons
7. Sources
8. Safety exclusions when personal identity is part of the public story

## Image handling

Do not store copied portraits in this repository. Link to official posters, official case pages, public court exhibits, or reputable news pages. Add a caption explaining the source and relevance.

## Safety exclusions

Do not add private addresses, private family details, non-public contact details, copied private images, or instructions for locating a person in real time. SpyWatch is an educational archive, not a live tracking product.
