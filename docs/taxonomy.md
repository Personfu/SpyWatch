# Taxonomy

SpyWatch classifies public-source actor research by sponsorship, mission, behavior, and confidence.

## Sponsorship categories

- `state-intelligence` — foreign intelligence service or military intelligence body.
- `state-aligned` — public reporting links activity to a state interest, while the command relationship remains unclear.
- `military-cyber` — cyber organization associated with a national military body.
- `hybrid-operations` — actor tied to influence, disruption, cyber activity, or proxy operations.
- `ecrime` — financially motivated criminal group.
- `hacktivist` — ideological or politically branded activity.
- `historic` — legacy organization useful for context.
- `counterintelligence` — defensive, law-enforcement, or military CI organization.

## Naming rule

Use the most widely recognized public name as `name`. Put vendor aliases and government names in `aliases`. Do not merge actors only because public reports describe similar behavior.

## Defensive viewpoint

Every profile should answer:

1. Who is the actor alleged or assessed to be?
2. Who tracks it under what names?
3. What sectors does public reporting mention?
4. What events define the actor in open reporting?
5. What defensive lessons should organizations take away?
6. What sources support the profile?
